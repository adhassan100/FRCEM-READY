Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the FRCEM SBA examination

The FRCEM SBA (Single Best Answer) exam is a written examination assessing theoretical knowledge and clinical expertise in emergency medicine. It replaced the FRCEM Final SAQ exam from August 2021 and is blueprinted against the RCEM 2021 Emergency Medicine curriculum.
The exam consists of 180 multiple-choice questions divided into two 90-minute papers. Questions cover the full range of RCEM 2021 curriculum SLOs including clinical management, critical appraisal, quality improvement, and leadership.
Yes. Both the FRCEM SBA and FRCEM OSCE must be passed independently to achieve FRCEM certification. There is no cross-compensation between components and both must be completed within seven years of each other. Candidates may sit them in any order.
For the 20 May 2026 exam, the application window is 18–25 February 2026 (results 24 June 2026). For the 7 October 2026 exam, the application window is 1–8 July 2026 (results 11 November 2026). Always check the official RCEM website for the most up-to-date information.
SLO5 (Injured Patients / Trauma) often comprises around 30 questions and is a high-yield area. SLO1 (Stable Patients) is also heavily represented. Non-clinical SLOs such as SLO10/11 (Critical Appraisal & QIP) are frequently underestimated but are regularly tested.
